# Curricular Proposal

## Strengthening Physical Education and *Vida Saludable* in Mexican Lower-Secondary Education

> A complement to the existing curriculum of the Nueva Escuela Mexicana (NEM) — Plan de Estudios 2022 — targeted at students aged 12–15.

---

## 1. Context and Justification

### 1.1 What already exists in the system

The NEM Plan 2022 includes:

- **Educación Física** as a subject — currently 2 hours per week in secondary education.
- **Vida Saludable** as an articulating axis (eje articulador) within the campo formativo *De lo humano y lo comunitario* — a transversal theme rather than a dedicated subject.

In practice (per consultation of the Plan 2022 and the Programa Sintético for lower secondary):

- Physical Education has been progressively reduced from up to 5 h/week (pre-2010) to 2 h/week today.
- *Vida Saludable* depends entirely on each school's interpretation; there is no standardized content, no specific teacher training package, no dedicated assessment.
- Recent SEP statements (2024–2025) acknowledge the gap and announce measures (banning ultraprocessed food from schools, promoting water, training cooperatives), but classroom-level reinforcement remains weak.

### 1.2 Why complement, not replace

Proposing a brand-new subject would require federal curricular reform, a politically improbable timeline. **Reinforcing existing slots** is faster, cheaper, and operationally realistic. It also respects the work teachers and the SEP have already invested in the NEM framework.

### 1.3 Why lower-secondary (12–15)

| Criterion | Evidence |
|-----------|----------|
| Higher prevalence | Adolescents 12–19 show 40.1 % overweight + obesity vs 36.6 % in 5–11 (ENSANUT 2020–2024) |
| Discretionary spending | Secondary students manage their own money and visit nanostores autonomously |
| Habit consolidation | Adolescent habits track strongly into adulthood (Singh et al., 2008) |
| Cognitive readiness | Students can engage in critical analysis of their food environment |
| Access | The validation includes the director of a secondary school (Sec. Téc. 78), providing a feasibility check |

## 2. Proposal Components

### 2.1 Component A — Extended Physical Education (3 h/week, up from 2)

**Structure:**

- **Session 1 (60 min):** Traditional physical activity (existing). Sport, motor skills, team play.
- **Session 2 (60 min):** Traditional physical activity (existing). Conditioning, fitness fundamentals.
- **Session 3 (60 min) — NEW:** *Active Lifestyle Literacy*. Indoor or outdoor, but with a structured cognitive component.

**Session 3 content rotates over a school year:**

| Week of cycle | Topic | Skill |
|---------------|-------|-------|
| 1 | Understanding nutrition labels | Read and interpret a label on a real product brought from a nanostore |
| 2 | Designing a healthy snack on a 20-peso budget | Math + nutrition + planning |
| 3 | Mapping the food environment around the school | Field exercise + geography + critical observation |
| 4 | Cooking demo with simple ingredients | Practical literacy (often a guest cook) |
| 5 | Heart-rate, effort and recovery | Linking physical activity with measurable feedback |
| 6 | Sleep, hydration and screen time | Holistic lifestyle |
| 7 | Marketing literacy: how ads are designed | Media literacy and critical thinking |
| 8 | Reflection + family conversation prep | Transferring learning home |

Cycles repeat with deeper content as the year progresses.

### 2.2 Component B — *Vida Saludable* Critical-Environment Module

Embedded in any subject that touches *Vida Saludable* (Geography, Civic Education, Natural Sciences).

**Activity:** every two weeks (30 min within an existing class), students audit one specific aspect of the food environment around their school:

- Count and classify products in 3 nearby nanostores.
- Document prices of healthy vs ultraprocessed options.
- Build a school-wide map of food access points.
- Present findings to other grades or to parents in a year-end assembly.

This connects abstract nutrition education to **their actual environment** and gives them agency. It also feeds back into the Phase-1 / Phase-2 analyses: future student cohorts could collect their own nanostore data, turning the school into a research partner.

### 2.3 Component C — Teacher Training Package

Without training, no intervention works. We propose:

- **8 hours of asynchronous CPD** for PE teachers and any teacher who will deliver *Vida Saludable* content.
- Modules: nutrition fundamentals, label literacy, NEM alignment, adolescent psychology of behavior change, classroom-management techniques for outdoor sessions.
- **Optional 4-hour synchronous workshop** with a sport-medicine or public-health practitioner.
- **Resource pack**: printable nutrition cards, label-reading worksheets, sample nanostore audit templates, a 30-question test bank.

### 2.4 Component D — Family Bridge

A monthly take-home activity that:

- Asks the family to discuss one dimension of the month's content.
- Includes a simple, low-cost recipe to try together.
- Returns a one-page reflection signed by parent and student.

This is not homework in the traditional sense — it is a structured invitation that respects family time constraints (recall: the dominant survey finding was that families lack time).

## 3. Alignment with the NEM Plan 2022

| NEM concept | How this proposal aligns |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| Campo formativo: *De lo humano y lo comunitario* | Component B explicitly anchored here |
| Eje articulador: *Vida Saludable* | All components feed this axis |
| Eje articulador: *Pensamiento crítico* | Critical-environment audits |
| Eje articulador: *Apropiación de las culturas a través de la lectura y la escritura* | Label literacy + family-reflection pages |
| Procesos de Desarrollo de Aprendizaje (PDA) | Each session ties to existing PDAs already in the *Programa Sintético* |

The proposal does **not** add new PDAs; it strengthens delivery of existing ones.

## 4. Implementation Pathway

| Step | Action | Time |
|------|--------|------|
| 1 | Pilot in 3 schools per state, lower-secondary level | 1 school year |
| 2 | Baseline measurement (BMI, knowledge survey, self-reported behaviour) | First 2 weeks |
| 3 | Continuous teacher support + monthly fidelity checks | Ongoing |
| 4 | Midpoint measurement | Month 5 |
| 5 | Endpoint measurement | Month 10 |
| 6 | Compare against matched control schools | Month 10–11 |
| 7 | Adjust → scale to all secondary schools of a state | Year 2 |

## 5. Cost Estimate (Order of Magnitude)

| Item | Estimated cost per school per year |
|------|-----------------------------------|
| Teacher CPD (one-time, amortized) | ≈ MXN 4,000 |
| Print materials (label cards, audit sheets) | ≈ MXN 2,500 |
| Optional cook / guest facilitator (3–4 visits) | ≈ MXN 3,000 |
| Coordination time | absorbed by existing roles |
| **Total per school per year** | **≈ MXN 9,500** |

Scaled to ~35,000 secondary schools in Mexico, the order-of-magnitude annual cost would be ~ MXN 330 million — under 0.05 % of the SEP federal education budget. We will refine this estimate after the validation interviews surface any missing cost categories.

---

## 6. Expected Outcomes

Quantitative projections are produced by the Phase-5 Monte Carlo simulation. Qualitative expected outcomes:

- Students gain functional literacy of food labels and nutrition.
- Students develop a critical lens on their food environment.
- Families have a structured channel of conversation around health.
- Teachers gain CPD recognition and concrete tools.
- The SEP gains a low-cost reinforcement of existing curricular axes without legislative changes.

---

## 7. Risks and Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Teacher resistance to added workload | Component C is delivered as paid CPD; Component A reuses existing PE slot + 1 extra hour, not a full new subject |
| Class-management challenges during outdoor sessions | Optional workshop in Component C addresses this directly |
| Family non-engagement with the bridge component | Family bridge is **invitation, not enforcement** — fidelity tracked but not punitive |
| Effects diluted across heterogeneous schools | Pilot in matched schools, with formal fidelity-of-implementation checks |
| The "null long-term result" found in some meta-analyses | Acknowledged openly in Phase 5; pilot design must include long enough follow-up |

---

## 8. What we will revise after expert validation

This proposal is the *pre-validation draft*. After the five expert interviews, the following sections are explicitly open to revision:

- **Hours per week** (Component A): could go from +1 h to +30 min if directors flag scheduling impossibility.
- **Critical-environment module** placement: which subject hosts it.
- **Teacher training format**: hours, mode, accreditation.
- **Family bridge frequency and design**.

The post-validation version is published as `docs/curricular_proposal_v2.md` and is the version that informs the paper's final intervention description.
